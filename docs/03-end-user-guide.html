1What is Clear Build USA?

Clear Build USA is a cloud-based business management platform built exclusively for US construction contractors. It puts everything you need to run your contracting business in one place:

  • Leads & Contacts - track every potential customer and manage your pipeline
  • Quotes - create professional estimates and get customer approval online
  • Projects - manage every active job with budgets, tasks, and team assignments
  • Invoices - send invoices and let customers pay online by card or bank transfer
  • Payments - full payment history with reversal support
  • Change Orders - document and get approval for any scope change
  • Scheduling - manage your availability and let customers book meetings
  • Communications - all customer messages in one inbox
  • Team Management - invite your crew and assign roles
  • AI Assistant - ask business questions, get instant answers
The big picture Clear Build USA replaces scattered spreadsheets, paper invoices, and multiple disconnected apps with one professional platform that makes your business look and run better.

2Setting Up Your Account

Step 1: Register

1

Go to clearbuildusa.com and click "Get Started Free"

You'll land on the registration page. Enter your first name, last name, email address, and choose a password (minimum 8 characters).

2

Check your email and click the verification link

We'll send a verification email to the address you registered with. Click the link inside to verify your account. You cannot log in until your email is verified. Check your spam folder if it doesn't arrive within 2 minutes.

3

Log in with your email and password

Go to /login and enter your credentials. The app will remember you for 15 days before asking you to log in again.

4

Set up your business

On first login, you'll be taken to Business Setup. Enter your business name and type (General Contractor, Plumber, Electrician, etc.). This creates your workspace - all your data is attached to this business.

5

You're in - start with Settings

Before creating your first quote, go to Settings → Business Profile and upload your logo and add your business address. This information appears on all your quotes and invoices.

Free Trial

You get a 15-day free trial with full access to every feature. No credit card required. After 15 days, upgrade to the Monthly ($49/month) or Yearly ($490/year) plan to continue. Your data is never deleted - if you upgrade later, everything is still there.

Forgot Your Password?

  1. Go to the login page and click "Forgot Password?"
  2. Enter your email address and click "Send Reset Link"
  3. Check your inbox for a reset link (valid for 1 hour)
  4. Click the link and enter your new password

1

Go to Contacts in the sidebar and click "New Contact" (top right)

Or click the quick-action button on the Dashboard.

2

Fill in the contact's details

Name and contact type are required. Email and phone are optional but recommended so you can send quotes and invoices directly from the app.

3

Choose the contact type and lead status

If they're a new potential customer, choose "Lead" and set status to "New Lead". You can always update this as they progress through your pipeline.

Converting a Lead to a Customer

When you win the job, open the lead's contact page and click "Convert to Customer". Their type changes to Customer and they appear in your Customers section. All their history (quotes, communications) stays intact.

Click any contact to open their detail page. Here you'll find:

  • At-a-Glance sidebar - contact info, type, lifetime value (total invoiced)

  • Quick actions - create a quote, send a message, schedule a meeting - all from the contact page

Customers Section

The Customers page shows all your won clients in a card grid. Each card shows the customer's name, number of invoices, and lifetime value. Toggle between grid and list view using the icons in the top right.

6Quotes

Quotes are professional job estimates you send to customers for approval. When a customer approves a quote, you can convert it to an invoice with one click.

Quote Status Flow

Draft→ Sent→ Approved or Rejected

Creating a Quote

1

Go to Quotes → New Quote

The app auto-assigns the next quote number (e.g. Q-0001). You can customise the prefix in Settings → Tax & Numbering.

2

Fill in the 3-column header

Customer - search and select from your contacts. Job Site - the address where the work will be done. Valid Through - the quote expiry date (defaults to 30 days from today).

3

Add your line items

For each item/service: enter a description, quantity, unit price, discount (optional), and tax rate (optional). The total calculates automatically.

4

Choose how to send it

Use the Send Channel toggles to select Email, SMS, or WhatsApp. Click "Send Quote" - the customer receives a link to view the professional quote preview.

How the Customer Sees the Quote

When you send a quote, the customer receives a link to a professional preview page (no login required). On that page they can:

  • Approve - marks the quote as Approved. You're notified and can convert it to an invoice.
  • Request Changes - customer can leave a note. You'll see it in the quote's activity feed.
Pro Tip - Convert to Invoice Once a quote is Approved, open it and click "Convert to Invoice." All line items are copied over - no double entry. The linked quote and invoice are automatically cross-referenced.

Quote Activity Sidebar

On the Quote detail page, the right sidebar shows the full activity history - when it was created, sent, viewed, and approved (or rejected). Use this to see exactly what happened and when.

Quote List View

The Quotes list shows 5 stat cards at the top (Total, Draft, Sent, Approved, Rejected) so you can see your pipeline at a glance. Filter by status using the tabs below the stat cards.

7Invoices

Invoices are payment requests sent to customers. Customers can pay online by card or bank transfer, or you can record a manual payment (check, cash) when you receive it.

Invoice Status Flow

Draft→ Sent→ Due→ Overdue→ Paid or Partial

Creating an Invoice

1

Go to Invoices → New Invoice (or convert from an approved quote)

Converting from a quote copies all line items automatically. Creating manually: the app auto-assigns the next invoice number (e.g. INV-0001).

2

Select the customer and link to a project (optional)

If you don't link to an existing project, the app automatically creates one for you and links the invoice to it.

3

Add line items

Same as a quote - description, quantity, unit price, discount, and tax. Totals calculate automatically.

4

Set payment options (right sidebar)

Choose the due date, payment method, and whether to schedule automatic reminders (e.g. "Send reminder 3 days before due via email").

5

Add internal notes (optional)

Notes are only visible to your team, never to the customer. Use this for internal job notes or payment terms reminders.

6

Send the invoice

Click "Send Invoice." The customer receives a link to view the invoice and pay online.

How the Customer Pays Online

The customer clicks the link in their email. They see a professional invoice page with your branding. At the bottom is a large "Pay $X" button. Clicking it takes them to a secure Stripe payment page where they can pay by:

  • Credit or debit card (Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Discover)
  • ACH bank transfer (US bank accounts)

When payment is complete, Stripe notifies Clear Build USA automatically. The invoice status updates to Paid and you receive an in-app notification.

Recording a Manual Payment

If the customer pays by check or cash, record it manually:

  1. Open the invoice
  2. Click "Record Payment" in the Payment Summary sidebar
  3. Choose the amount tab: Full / 50% / Deposit / Custom
  4. Select the payment method: Card, Bank Transfer, Check, or Cash
  5. Click "Record Payment" to save
Partial Payments If a customer pays a deposit (e.g. 50% upfront), record the first payment. The invoice status changes to "Partial." Record the remaining payment when you receive it - the invoice moves to "Paid" when the balance reaches $0.

Invoice Detail Page

The Invoice detail page shows:

  • Left panel: Invoice header, line items, totals, notes, activity timeline
  • Right sidebar: Payment summary with progress bar (amount paid vs. total), and "Record Payment" / "Manage Billing" buttons

8Projects

Projects are the jobs you're actively working on. Each project tracks budget, timeline, team, tasks, and linked financial documents (quotes, invoices, change orders).

Project Statuses

Planning→ In Progress→ On Hold→ Completed or Cancelled

Creating a Project

1

Go to Projects → New Project

Auto-assigned project number (e.g. PRJ-0001). Customise the prefix in Settings → Tax & Numbering.

2

Fill in project details

Project name, select the customer, start date, end date, and budget amount. The app automatically calculates "Day X of Y" from your dates.

3

Set budget and deposit

Enter the total project budget. Set a deposit percentage if you require an upfront payment (e.g. 30%). This helps track whether a deposit has been received.

4

Assign team members

Use the Team panel to assign specific crew members from your team to this project. They'll see the project in their view.

5

Set client visibility

Toggle "Visible to client" if you want the customer to be able to see project progress. When enabled, the customer can view tasks and updates (future feature).

Managing Tasks

Inside a project, the Tasks tab lets you break the job down into individual steps:

  • Click "Add Task" and enter a task name and due date
  • Assign the task to a specific team member
  • Mark tasks complete as work progresses - the project progress bar updates automatically
  • Tasks are visible to the whole team (based on their role)

Project List View

The Projects list shows colour-coded cards for each project. Each card shows:

  • Project name and customer
  • Progress bar (% of tasks complete)
  • "Day X of Y" timeline indicator
  • Status badge (In Progress, Planning, etc.)
  • Budget amount

Logging Project Updates

Use Project Updates (sidebar → Project Updates) to log daily progress notes. These serve as a running diary of the job - useful for client reporting and dispute resolution if needed.

Item Requirements

The Item Requirements section (sidebar) lets you create material lists linked to projects. Track what materials are needed, quantities, estimated costs, and procurement status. This helps with job costing and ordering.

What You'll See on the Payments Page

  • 4 method stat cards - total received by Card, Bank Transfer, Check, and Cash
  • Mini bar chart - payments over the last 6 months at a glance
  • Payment list - every payment with date, amount, invoice, and method

Payment Methods

MethodHow it's recorded

🏦 Bank TransferAutomatically via Stripe ACH, or manually when you receive a bank transfer
📝 CheckAlways manual - record when you receive and deposit the check

Reversing a Payment

If you recorded a payment by mistake (e.g. wrong amount, bounced check), you can reverse it:

  1. Find the payment in the Payments list
  2. Click the three-dot menu (⋯) on the right
  3. Select "Reverse Payment"
  4. Confirm - the payment is removed and the invoice's outstanding balance is restored
Reversal is Owner/Admin only Only users with the Owner or Admin role can reverse payments. Staff and Crew members will not see the Reverse option.

10Change Orders

A Change Order is an official document for any work that was added or changed after the original quote was agreed. Instead of verbal agreements that lead to disputes, Change Orders give you a written, customer-approved paper trail.

Change Order Status Flow

Draft→ Sent→ Approved or Rejected or Void

Creating a Change Order

1

Go to Change Orders → New Change Order

Link it to the relevant project. Add a description explaining why the scope is changing.

2

Add line items for the new/changed work

Include description, quantity, and unit price for each item being added. The total calculates automatically.

3

Send to the customer

Click "Send Change Order." The customer receives a link to review and approve (or reject) the change.

4

Wait for approval - then proceed with the work

Never start additional work until the change order is Approved. The approval is your written authorisation.

Best Practice Get every scope change - even small ones - documented as a change order. "It'll only take an hour" disputes cost contractors thousands. A 2-minute change order prevents them.

12Communications

The Communications section is your unified message inbox - every email, SMS, WhatsApp, phone note, and log entry for every contact, all in one place.

Layout

  • Left panel: contact list, search bar, channel filter tabs (All, Email, SMS, WhatsApp, Note)
  • Right panel: the conversation thread for the selected contact

Sending a Message

  1. Click on a contact in the left panel
  2. In the message input at the bottom, choose the channel (Email, SMS, WhatsApp)
  3. Type your message and click Send

Logging a Communication

If you had a phone call or in-person meeting, log it manually:

  1. Click "Log Communication" in the top right of the chat view
  2. Choose the channel (Call, Note, etc.), enter a subject if needed, and write the summary
  3. Save - it appears in the contact's conversation history with a timestamp
Why log calls and meetings? A complete communication history protects you if a dispute arises ("You said you'd do X on that call"). Every logged interaction is date-stamped and searchable.

14Templates & Notifications

Templates

Templates are reusable message bodies for common communications - quote-sent messages, invoice reminders, payment confirmations, etc.

  • Go to Templates in the sidebar to see your library
  • Click "New Template" to create one - give it a name, choose a type, write the subject and body
  • Use variables like {{contact_name}} and {{business_name}} - these are filled in automatically when you send
  • Duplicate any template to create a variation quickly

Default templates are pre-loaded: Quote Sent, Invoice Due Reminder, Payment Received. Customise them with your own wording in Settings → Email Templates.

Notifications

The Notifications section (bell icon) shows in-app alerts for events like quote approvals, invoice payments, and team invites. Two tabs:

  • Activity - your notification feed. Click "Mark all read" to clear the count.
  • Templates - manage notification message templates

To send a manual notification to a customer (e.g. a reminder email or WhatsApp message), go to Notifications, click "Send Notification," choose a template and the contact, and send. Owner or Admin role required.

19Frequently Asked Questions

Getting Started

Why didn't I receive the verification email?
Check your spam or junk folder first. If it's not there, go to the login page, click "Didn't receive the email?" and request a new one. Make sure you used the correct email address when registering.
Can I have more than one business on one account?
Yes. One Clear Build USA account can be a member of multiple businesses. Use the business switcher in the top bar to switch between them. Each business has its own separate data, contacts, invoices, and settings.
What happens when my free trial ends?
After 15 days, your account enters "restricted mode." You can still see all your data, but creating new quotes, invoices, and projects will prompt you to upgrade. Your data is never deleted - upgrade at any time to regain full access.

Quotes & Invoices

Can I change a quote after I've sent it?
Yes, but be careful - if the customer has already received the link, any changes you make will be reflected in their view the next time they open it. Best practice: if significant changes are needed, void the current quote, create a new revised version, and send the new link.
How does online invoice payment work - when do I get the money?
When a customer pays by card, Stripe typically deposits the funds to your bank account within 2 business days. ACH bank transfers take 3–5 business days. You can see your deposit schedule in the Stripe dashboard (linked from Settings → Integrations).
Can a customer pay a partial amount on an invoice?
Yes. You can record partial payments manually (50%, deposit %, or any custom amount). For online payments, the full invoice amount is charged - for partial billing, create separate invoices or record the partial payment manually after the fact.
The customer approved a quote but I need to change some items before invoicing. What do I do?
Convert the quote to an invoice, then edit the invoice's line items as needed. If the change is significant (scope or price), issue a Change Order for the difference so there's a documented trail of why the amounts differ from the original quote.
How do I customise my invoice/quote number format?
Go to Settings → Tax & Numbering. You can change the prefix for quotes (default: Q-), invoices (default: INV-), and projects (default: PRJ-). The number continues sequentially from the last one created. You cannot retroactively renumber past documents.

Projects & Payments

I created an invoice without linking a project - where did it go?
Clear Build automatically creates a project for you and links the invoice to it. Check your Projects list - you'll find a new project named after the invoice. You can rename and add details to it.
Can I track time worked on a project?
The current version supports task tracking (with due dates and completion status) and project updates (daily notes). Dedicated time/hour tracking per team member is planned for a future release.
I recorded a payment for the wrong invoice. How do I fix it?
Reverse the payment on the incorrect invoice (open that invoice → Payments section → Reverse), then record it on the correct invoice. You need Owner or Admin role to reverse payments.

Team & Permissions

A team member invited someone but they haven't signed up yet. Can I resend the invite?
Yes. Go to Team, find the pending invite in the list, and click "Resend Invite." The invite link is valid for 7 days from the time it was sent (or re-sent).
If I remove a team member, do they lose access right away?
Yes - immediately. Even if they are currently logged in, their next action in the app will log them out. This is by design to protect your business data.
Can a Staff member see financial data?
Staff can view and edit quotes, invoices, and projects. They cannot record payments, reverse payments, or send bulk notifications. Crew members have even more limited access - they can only see assigned projects and tasks.

Account & Security

How do I change my password?
Go to Settings → Security. Enter your current password and then your new password (minimum 8 characters). Or, if you've forgotten your password, use the "Forgot Password" link on the login page.
Is my business data private from other businesses on the platform?
Yes, completely. Every piece of data (contacts, quotes, invoices, projects) is isolated to your business. No other business can see your data, and you cannot see theirs. This isolation is enforced at every level of the system.
How long does the system keep me logged in?
Your login session lasts 15 days. After that, you'll be asked to log in again. If you check "Keep me signed in" on the login page (if available), the session extends further.

Billing

How do I cancel my subscription?
Go to /subscription → click "Manage Billing." This opens the Stripe billing portal where you can cancel your subscription. Cancellation takes effect at the end of your current billing period - you keep full access until then.
Can I get a refund?
Contact support (see Section 20) within 7 days of a charge if you believe there's been an error. We review each case individually. We do not offer prorated refunds for mid-period cancellations.
Is there a mobile app?
The web app is fully mobile-responsive and works on any smartphone browser. A dedicated iOS and Android app is on the roadmap for later in 2026. For now, you can add the website to your home screen for a near-app experience.

Before You Contact Support - Quick Checklist

  1. Search the FAQ in the Help section - most common issues are covered there
  2. Try asking the AI chatbot - it can walk you through most features step by step
  3. Try logging out and logging back in - this resolves most session-related issues
  4. Check that your subscription is active (Settings → Subscription) if features are locked
  5. If a quote or invoice isn't displaying correctly, try refreshing the page

When Submitting a Support Ticket, Include

  • Your business name and email address
  • The name of the feature or page where the issue occurred (e.g. "Invoice INV-0023")
  • What you were trying to do and what happened instead
  • A screenshot if possible - this speeds up diagnosis significantly
